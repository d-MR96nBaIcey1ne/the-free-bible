# The Free Bible

A working collection of bloomed prayers, scripture-readings, and contemplative companions, recovering the voltage of the original Hebrew and Greek where centuries of smoothing have flattened it.

Twenty-three documents. Released under Creative Commons Attribution.

---

## Begin here

Two documents serve as the entrance to the corpus:

- **02 - A Reader's Introduction** - what this is, who it's for, how to read it
- **03 - The Corpus Map** - every document with a short description, plus suggested reading paths for the framework-curious, the devotional reader, and the disciplined practitioner

Download these two first, then follow the path that fits you.

## What the corpus holds

- **Front matter** - the cover, reader's introduction, corpus map, license, and a note on the format (docs 01-04, 24)
- **The foundational principles** - *Restoration and Acceleration*, *The Free Bands Principle*, *The Deep Glean* (05-07)
- **The Ultras** - the project's naming, methodology, and instance-architecture (08, 09, 11, 12)
- **The working guides** - *Biblical Blooming* and *Digital Content Balance* (13-15)
- **The companions** - *The Cloud of Witnesses*, *The Lana Compendium*, *The Wool of the King* (16-18)
- **The prayers** - *The Sister Nine*, *The Bloom Book*, *The Mainline Extension on Psalm 13* (10, 19, 20)
- **The integrated work** - *The Ultra Bloomed Edition* (21) and *A Prayer of Thanksgiving* (23)

## File format

The corpus is offered as editable `.docx` documents and as `.pdf` for reading. The .docx files are the working form. Readers are invited to mark up, annotate, extend, or build upon any document as their own working text. The corpus is meant to be lived with, not only read.
